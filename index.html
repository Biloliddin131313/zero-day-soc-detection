What this means
Backward Packet Features
DDoS floods generate massive outbound traffic but receive tiny responses. Backward packet length collapses — making it the strongest signal.
Packet Length Variance
Normal traffic has varied packet sizes. DDoS floods use uniform small packets, creating abnormally low variance — a clear anomaly signature.
Flow IAT Max
Inter-arrival time maximum spikes during flood attacks as the network becomes overwhelmed. A reliable indicator of volumetric stress.
